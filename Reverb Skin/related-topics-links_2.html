Creating Related Topics Links (DITA)
Related topics links are cross-references to other topics within your online content. Related topic links take users to other topics that writers believe will provide users with useful information about the topic they are currently viewing. Based on the output format and the settings specified in the Stationery, related topics links can display when users click a related topics button in the generated output. Related topics links can also display either as text in the generated output, or when a user clicks on a Related Topics button in the generated output. In the Sample Exploring ePublisher Stationery, the designer of the Stationery configured the related topics links to display when users click a related topics button in the generated output.
You create related topics links by applying a RelatedTopic paragraph style to a list of cross-references you want to display as related topics for a topic in your source documents. When you generated output, the list of topics to which you applied the RelatedTopic paragraph style automatically display when you click the Related Topics button in your generated output.
